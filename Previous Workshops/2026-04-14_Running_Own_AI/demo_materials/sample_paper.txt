Topological Phase Transitions in Monolayer Jacutingaite (Pt2HgSe3):
A Kane-Mele Analysis with Spin-Orbit and Rashba Corrections

Authors: A. Nakamura, R. Chen, and P. Oliveira
Department of Physics, University of California, Berkeley, CA 94720, USA

========================================================================
ABSTRACT
========================================================================

Monolayer jacutingaite (Pt2HgSe3) has recently emerged as a candidate
quantum spin Hall insulator with a sizable topological gap. In this work,
we investigate the topological phase diagram of monolayer Pt2HgSe3 using
an extended Kane-Mele model that incorporates intrinsic spin-orbit
coupling, Rashba spin-orbit coupling, and a staggered sublattice
potential. We compute the Z2 invariant across a wide parameter space and
identify three distinct phases: a quantum spin Hall (QSH) insulator, a
trivial band insulator, and a metallic regime at the phase boundaries. Our
calculations, which are based on density functional theory parameters
fitted to first-principles band structures, reveal that the topological
gap can be tuned from 0.15 eV to closure by applying moderate biaxial
strain of approximately 4-6%. We further show that the Rashba coupling,
while not destroying the QSH phase, shifts the critical strain at which
the topological transition occurs. The phase boundaries exhibit a
characteristic funnel-shaped topology in the strain-Rashba parameter space
that has not been previously reported for this material. These findings
suggest that monolayer jacutingaite offers a robust and experimentally
tunable platform for studying topological phase transitions, which makes
it a pretty interesting system for potential spintronic applications.

========================================================================
1. INTRODUCTION
========================================================================

The discovery of topological insulators has fundamentally reshaped our
understanding of quantum phases of matter [1]. Unlike conventional band
insulators, topological insulators possess a bulk energy gap accompanied
by gapless edge or surface states that are protected by time-reversal
symmetry [2,3]. In two dimensions, the quantum spin Hall (QSH) effect
gives rise to helical edge modes that carry spin-polarized currents
without dissipation, making these materials attractive for spintronic
and quantum computing applications.

The Kane-Mele model, originally proposed for graphene [4], provides the
minimal theoretical framework for understanding the QSH effect on a
honeycomb lattice. In this model, intrinsic spin-orbit coupling (SOC)
opens a topological gap at the Dirac points, while the Rashba SOC, which
arises from broken structural inversion symmetry, competes with the
intrinsic SOC and can close the topological gap when sufficiently strong.
Despite the elegance of this theoretical framework, graphene's intrinsic
SOC is vanishingly small (~1 microeV), rendering the QSH effect
unobservable in practice.

The search for materials with large intrinsic SOC on a honeycomb lattice
has led to the identification of several candidate QSH insulators,
including bismuthene on SiC substrates [5], stanene [6], and various
transition metal dichalcogenides [7]. Among these, monolayer jacutingaite
(Pt2HgSe3) has attracted considerable attention following its theoretical
prediction as a Kane-Mele-type QSH insulator with a topological gap
exceeding 0.15 eV [8,9]. The material's honeycomb arrangement of Hg
atoms, combined with the strong SOC of the Pt and Hg atoms, produces a
topological gap two orders of magnitude larger than that of bismuthene.

Recent experimental advances have enabled the synthesis and mechanical
exfoliation of jacutingaite crystals [10], bringing the experimental
realization of the QSH effect in this material within reach. However,
several questions remain regarding the robustness of the topological phase
under realistic perturbations, including strain, substrate interactions,
and disorder. In particular, the interplay between strain-induced
modifications to the hopping parameters and the Rashba coupling arising
from substrate effects has not been systematically explored.

In this paper, we present a comprehensive study of the topological phase
diagram of monolayer Pt2HgSe3 using an extended Kane-Mele model
parametrized by first-principles calculations. We map out the full
strain-Rashba phase diagram and identify the critical conditions for the
topological phase transition. Our results provide quantitative guidance
for experimental efforts to observe and control topological phase
transitions in this material.

========================================================================
2. MODEL AND METHODS
========================================================================

We describe monolayer Pt2HgSe3 within an extended Kane-Mele model on the
honeycomb lattice. The full Hamiltonian reads

  $\hat{H}$ = H_0 + H_SOC + H_R + H_Delta

where the individual terms are given by:

  H_0 = -t sum_{<i,j>,sigma} c^dagger_{i,sigma} c_{j,sigma}

is the nearest-neighbor tight-binding Hamiltonian with hopping amplitude
t = 1.12 eV obtained from Wannier function fitting to our DFT band
structure.

The intrinsic spin-orbit coupling term is

  H_SOC = i * lambda_SOC sum_{<<i,j>>,sigma,sigma'} nu_{ij}
          c^dagger_{i,sigma} s^z_{sigma,sigma'} c_{j,sigma'}

where lambda_SOC = 0.078 eV is the intrinsic SOC strength, nu_{ij} =
+/-1 depending on the orientation of the next-nearest-neighbor hopping
path, and s^z is the z-component Pauli matrix.

The Rashba term takes the form

  H_R = i * lambda_R sum_{<i,j>,sigma,sigma'} c^dagger_{i,sigma}
        (s x d_hat_{ij})^z_{sigma,sigma'} c_{j,sigma'}

where lambda_R is the Rashba coupling strength, s is the vector of Pauli
matrices, and d_hat_{ij} is the unit vector pointing from site j to site
i.

Finally, the staggered sublattice potential is

  H_Delta = Delta sum_{i,sigma} xi_i c^dagger_{i,sigma} c_{i,sigma}

where xi_i = +/-1 for the A/B sublattice and Delta parametrizes the
substrate-induced asymmetry.

We compute the Z2 topological invariant using the method of Fu and Kane
[11], evaluating the Pfaffian of the sewing matrix at the four
time-reversal invariant momenta (TRIM) points in the Brillouin zone. The
topological phase boundary is determined by the gap-closing condition at
the K and K' points. We parametrize strain through a linear modification
of the hopping amplitude: t(epsilon) = t_0 * exp(-beta * epsilon), where
epsilon is the biaxial strain and beta = 3.37 is the Gruneisen parameter
obtained from our DFT calculations.

First-principles calculations were performed using the Vienna Ab initio
Simulation Package (VASP) [12] with the Perdew-Burke-Ernzerhof (PBE)
exchange-correlation functional [13]. SOC was included self-consistently.
We used a plane-wave cutoff of 500 eV and a 15 x 15 x 1 Gamma-centered
k-mesh with a vacuum spacing of 20 Angstroms.

========================================================================
3. RESULTS
========================================================================

Figure 1 presents the topological phase diagram of monolayer Pt2HgSe3 in
the (epsilon, lambda_R) parameter space for fixed Delta = 0. The phase
diagram reveals three distinct regions: (i) the QSH phase (Z2 = 1) at
small strain and moderate Rashba coupling, (ii) a trivial insulator phase
(Z2 = 0) at large tensile strain, and (iii) a metallic phase (gap = 0)
along the phase boundaries.

At zero Rashba coupling (lambda_R = 0), the topological gap closes at a
critical tensile strain of epsilon_c = 5.2%, consistent with previous
first-principles predictions [8]. The gap closure occurs at the K point
and is accompanied by a band inversion between the Hg s-orbital and Se
p-orbital derived states. The critical strain is remarkably insensitive to
the value of Delta for Delta < 0.05 eV, indicating that moderate
substrate-induced asymmetry does not significantly affect the topological
stability.

When Rashba coupling is introduced, the phase boundary shifts to lower
critical strains. At lambda_R = 0.02 eV, the critical strain decreases
to epsilon_c = 4.1%, while at lambda_R = 0.05 eV it further reduces to
epsilon_c = 2.8%. The phase boundary traces a funnel-shaped curve in the
(epsilon, lambda_R) plane, narrowing the topological region as Rashba
coupling increases. This funnel topology can be understood analytically
from the gap-closing condition at K:

  E_gap(K) = 2 * |3*sqrt(3)*lambda_SOC - Delta| - 6*lambda_R

which shows that the Rashba coupling linearly reduces the effective gap
while the intrinsic SOC and sublattice potential compete.

The edge state spectrum, computed for a zigzag nanoribbon of width W = 100
unit cells, confirms the topological character of the QSH phase. Within
the bulk gap, a single pair of helical edge states connects the valence
and conduction bands, with a characteristic Dirac-like crossing at the
Gamma point of the one-dimensional Brillouin zone. The edge state
penetration depth is approximately xi = 3.2 nm at zero strain, increasing
to xi = 12.7 nm near the topological transition, consistent with the
expected divergence of the penetration depth at the critical point.

We additionally computed the spin Chern number C_s across the phase
diagram and verified that C_s = 1 throughout the QSH region, confirming
the Z2 classification. The spin Chern number exhibits a sharp jump from
C_s = 1 to C_s = 0 at the phase boundary, with no intermediate values
observed.

========================================================================
4. DISCUSSION AND CONCLUSION
========================================================================

Our results establish a comprehensive picture of the topological phase
diagram of monolayer jacutingaite under strain and Rashba perturbations.
The funnel-shaped phase boundary in the (epsilon, lambda_R) parameter
space provides clear quantitative predictions for experimental
verification. The critical strain of 5.2% at zero Rashba coupling falls
within the range achievable through flexible substrate techniques or
piezoelectric strain control, suggesting that continuous tuning across
the topological phase transition is experimentally feasible.

The robustness of the QSH phase against moderate Rashba coupling is
encouraging for practical applications, as substrate interactions
inevitably introduce some degree of inversion symmetry breaking. Our
finding that the topological gap remains open up to lambda_R ~ 0.06 eV
indicates that a variety of substrates could be used without destroying
the topological character.

An important implication of our work is the prediction that the optical
conductivity should exhibit a characteristic step-like feature at the
topological transition, arising from the van Hove singularity associated
with the gap closure. This optical signature could serve as an
experimentally accessible probe of the topological phase boundary,
complementing transport measurements of the edge conductance.

Furthermore, our analysis of the disorder-averaged topological invariant
demonstrates that the QSH phase remains stable against Anderson disorder
up to a critical disorder strength of W_c = 0.8 eV, corresponding to
approximately 70% of the bulk gap. This substantial robustness against
disorder further supports the viability of monolayer jacutingaite as an
experimental platform.

In conclusion, we have mapped out the full topological phase diagram of
monolayer Pt2HgSe3 using an extended Kane-Mele model parametrized by
first-principles calculations. The $\mathcal{H}$ exhibits rich phase
behavior controlled by strain and Rashba coupling, with a characteristic
funnel-shaped phase boundary. Our results provide concrete, quantitative
targets for experimental realization and manipulation of topological
phase transitions in this promising material.
