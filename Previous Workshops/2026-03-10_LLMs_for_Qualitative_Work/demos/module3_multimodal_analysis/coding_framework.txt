================================================================================
VISUAL CODING FRAMEWORK: VAPING CONTENT ANALYSIS
Adapted from Xu et al. (2024) JMIR Study
================================================================================

This coding scheme is used to systematically analyze visual elements in vaping
content. Use this framework alongside the Gemini prompts to code images and
video. Compare your codes (and Gemini's inferred codes) against these categories.

================================================================================
CODE 1: SETTING TYPE
================================================================================

Definition: The physical location and social environment depicted in the image.

Categories:

[S1] PRIVATE INDOOR SETTINGS
  - Bedroom, apartment, dorm room
  - Home or personal space
  - Implication: Intimate, personal, private activity
  - Normalization cue: Vaping presented as comfortable home activity

[S2] PUBLIC INDOOR SETTINGS
  - Coffee shops, bars, clubs, restaurants, offices, schools
  - Shared enclosed space with others (or designed for others to enter)
  - Implication: Activity acceptable in social/professional spaces
  - Normalization cue: Integration into everyday social institutions

[S3] OUTDOOR/NATURAL SETTINGS
  - Parks, streets, beaches, nature, open air
  - Outdoor social gathering spaces
  - Implication: Vaping as outdoor recreational activity
  - Normalization cue: Associated with leisure, freedom, social activities

[S4] SEMI-PUBLIC PERFORMANCES
  - Stages, studios, controlled filming location
  - Deliberately constructed environment for an audience
  - Implication: Vaping as performance or display
  - Normalization cue: Vaping as skill-based or entertainment activity

[S5] TRANSPORTATION/MOBILE
  - Cars, public transit, bikes, skateboards
  - "On-the-go" contexts
  - Implication: Vaping as portable, always-accessible activity
  - Normalization cue: Integrated with mobility and convenience

[S6] SYMBOLIC/ABSTRACT BACKGROUND
  - Nondescript walls, blurred background, digital effects
  - Focus is on person/device, not setting
  - Implication: Vaping isolated from everyday context
  - Normalization cue: Product-focused, lifestyle abstracted

CODING NOTE: For Gemini analysis, ask: "What setting is shown? What does that
suggest about when vaping is 'normal' or acceptable?"

================================================================================
CODE 2: VISUAL AESTHETICS & STYLE
================================================================================

Definition: How the image is visually styled, filtered, and composed to create
a particular aesthetic appeal.

Categories:

[V1] FILTER/EFFECT INTENSITY
  LOW: Minimal or no filters; appears documentary or raw
    → Implication: Authenticity, unmediated reality
    → Normalization: "This is how vaping really looks/is"

  MODERATE: Subtle filters (slight saturation boost, warmth)
    → Implication: Curated but naturalistic
    → Normalization: "Vaping is appealing in normal conditions"

  HIGH: Heavy filters, color grading, artistic effects, slow-motion
    → Implication: Aestheticized, aspirational, stylized
    → Normalization: "Vaping is beautiful, cool, artistic"

[V2] COLOR PALETTE
  - Warm (reds, oranges, yellows, golden hour): Cozy, intimate, energetic
  - Cool (blues, purples, teals, neon): Edgy, modern, futuristic
  - Saturated (vivid, intense colors): Eye-catching, exciting
  - Desaturated (muted, grayscale): Artistic, melancholic, nostalgic
  - Bright/High-contrast: Dynamic, striking
  - Dark/Low-contrast: Moody, mysterious, exclusive

[V3] LIGHTING
  - Natural/Golden hour: Aspirational, lifestyle-positive
  - Studio/Professional: High-quality, polished, premium
  - Backlighting/Silhouettes: Dramatic, mysterious, cool-factor
  - Harsh/Shadows: Edgy, darker tone
  - Ambient/Social setting: Casual, integration into normal spaces

[V4] COMPOSITION & FRAMING
  - Close-up on device: Product-focused, technical interest
  - Close-up on face/cloud: Aesthetic/performative interest
  - Wide shot: Social/environmental context important
  - Rule of thirds: Professional, intentional composition
  - Symmetrical: Planned, controlled aesthetic
  - Asymmetrical/Dynamic: Movement, energy, spontaneity

[V5] TEXT & GRAPHIC OVERLAYS
  - Hashtags (#vape, #vapers, #vapelife): Community markers
  - Product names/branding: Emphasis on product identity
  - Lifestyle language ("live free", "cloud life"): Aspirational framing
  - Skill-based language ("tricks", "competition"): Performance framing
  - Humorous/Meme elements: Social bonding, levity
  - Health warnings/disclaimers: Educational/cautionary framing (rare)

CODING NOTE: For Gemini analysis, ask: "Describe the visual style. What mood
or feeling does this create? How does the aesthetics shape perception of vaping?"

================================================================================
CODE 3: PRODUCT VISIBILITY & FRAMING
================================================================================

Definition: How prominently vaping devices and products are shown and framed.

Categories:

[P1] PRODUCT PROMINENCE
  PRIMARY: Device is central focus of image
    - Clear, sharp, well-lit device
    - Size and position draw eye first
    - Implication: Product is important, worth attention
    → Normalization: "The device is worth admiring"

  SECONDARY: Device visible but not primary focus
    - Clear but smaller, or slightly out of focus
    - Part of a larger scene
    - Implication: Normal prop in everyday scene
    → Normalization: "Vaping device is just another object"

  TERTIARY: Device barely visible or obscured
    - Small, blurred, partially hidden
    - Background element
    - Implication: Activity matters more than device
    → Normalization: "Focus is on the person/activity, not the tool"

[P2] DEVICE TYPE (if identifiable)
  - Pen-style vape: Sleek, portable, youth-oriented
  - Box mod: Technical, customizable, enthusiast-focused
  - Pod system: Modern, convenient, mainstream
  - Disposable: Casual, low-commitment
  - Unclear/Generic: Could be any type

[P3] BRAND VISIBILITY
  BRANDED: Brand name, logo, or distinctive product design visible
    → Normalization: Brand recognition, product-specific appeal

  GENERIC: Device visible but no identifiable brand
    → Normalization: Focus is on activity/culture, not brand

  OBSCURED: Device intentionally hidden or blurred
    → Normalization: Avoiding brand identification (sometimes for regulatory reasons)

[P4] CLOUD/VAPOR PROMINENCE
  LARGE/VISIBLE: Prominent vapor clouds, often in bright/artistic ways
    → Normalization: Aesthetic and visual spectacle of vaping

  MODERATE: Clouds visible but not emphasized
    → Normalization: Normal incidental aspect

  MINIMAL/ABSENT: Little to no visible vapor
    → Normalization: Focus on device/person, not consumption

CODING NOTE: For Gemini analysis, ask: "What vaping device or product is shown?
How prominent is it? What does the prominence suggest about the product's importance?"

================================================================================
CODE 4: SOCIAL CONTEXT
================================================================================

Definition: Who is depicted and what their social relationship to vaping appears to be.

Categories:

[C1] SOCIAL STRUCTURE
  SOLITARY: One person alone, vaping
    - Implication: Personal choice, individual activity
    - Normalization: "Vaping is something you do for yourself"

  DYADIC: Two people, one or both vaping
    - Implication: Shared activity, bonding
    - Normalization: "Vaping brings people together"

  GROUP: 3+ people, one or more vaping
    - Implication: Social/party activity, cultural norm within peer group
    - Normalization: "Everyone vapes; it's the norm in this group"

[C2] SOCIAL SETTING
  INTIMATE: Private, romantic, close personal context
    - Implication: Vaping as part of intimate relationship/moments

  FRIENDSHIP: Peers/friends socializing
    - Implication: Peer bonding, group belonging

  PARTY/CELEBRATION: Event-based, festive context
    - Implication: Vaping as celebration activity

  PROFESSIONAL/WORK: Workplace or professional context
    - Implication: Vaping as normalized in work/professional contexts

  PERFORMANCE/AUDIENCE: Intentional display for others
    - Implication: Vaping as skill, entertainment, status display

[C3] AGE APPARENT
  YOUTH (13-19): Teens
  YOUNG ADULT (20-30): College/early career age
  ADULT (30+): Adult age group
  MIXED: Multiple age groups depicted
  UNCLEAR: Cannot determine

[C4] EMOTIONAL TONE OF SOCIAL INTERACTION
  JOYFUL: Smiling, laughing, celebratory
    → Normalization: "Vaping makes people happy"

  CASUAL/RELAXED: Calm, comfortable, unbothered
    → Normalization: "Vaping is normal and low-key"

  ENERGETIC/EXCITED: Active, dynamic, enthusiastic
    → Normalization: "Vaping is exciting and fun"

  COOL/ASPIRATIONAL: Stylish, fashionable, status-seeking
    → Normalization: "Vaping is cool and desirable"

  NEUTRAL: No clear emotional expression

  ISOLATED/ALONE TONE: Even in groups, person seems separate
    → Normalization: "Vaping is a solitary activity within social settings"

CODING NOTE: For Gemini analysis, ask: "Who is shown? Are they alone or with others?
What is the social relationship to vaping being portrayed?"

================================================================================
CODE 5: TEXT & CAPTION FRAMING
================================================================================

Definition: How written language (captions, hashtags, overlays) frames vaping
in the image's context.

Categories:

[T1] FRAME TYPE
  LIFESTYLE: Language emphasizing vaping as identity/lifestyle
    Examples: #vapelife, #vapeculture, "living my best life"
    → Normalization: Vaping is core identity

  PRODUCT/REVIEW: Informational language about device, features, quality
    Examples: "Best vape for clouds", "pod review", comparison language
    → Normalization: Vaping is technical choice, consumer product

  HUMOR/MEME: Joking, self-aware, comedic language
    Examples: Puns, irony, memes, jokes about vaping
    → Normalization: Vaping treated lightly, normalized through humor

  EDUCATIONAL/HARM-REDUCTION: Information about safety, health, risks
    Examples: "Vaping is less harmful than smoking", health facts
    → Normalization: Vaping positioned as health-conscious choice

  SOCIAL/COMMUNITY: Language emphasizing belonging, friendship, shared identity
    Examples: "Shoutout to my vape squad", "vapers unite"
    → Normalization: Vaping as group identity, social belonging

  SKILL/PERFORMANCE: Language about tricks, competition, mastery
    Examples: "Attempting a new trick", "competition ready"
    → Normalization: Vaping as skilled activity worth mastery

  NO TEXT/CAPTION: Image without written language
    → Implication: Visual content speaks for itself

[T2] TONE OF LANGUAGE
  - Aspirational/Positive: Uplifting, exciting, desirable
  - Neutral/Informational: Factual, descriptive
  - Humorous: Joking, self-deprecating, ironic
  - Cautionary/Critical: Warning, educational, risk-aware
  - Mixed: Multiple tones present

[T3] AUDIENCE ADDRESSED
  - Peers/Youth: Language suggesting young audience
  - Consumers: Product-purchase-focused language
  - Enthusiasts: Technical, experienced vaper language
  - Health-conscious: Emphasis on harm reduction or health
  - General audience: Neutral, accessible language

CODING NOTE: For Gemini analysis, ask: "What text or captions are visible?
How do they frame vaping? What audience are they speaking to?"

================================================================================
CODE 6: NORMALIZATION CUES (SUMMARY)
================================================================================

Definition: Overall assessment of how the image normalizes vaping as an
everyday, acceptable, desirable activity.

Categories:

[N1] NORMALIZATION STRENGTH

  STRONG NORMALIZATION:
    - Vaping integrated seamlessly into everyday, desirable activities
    - High aesthetic appeal with positive emotional tone
    - Social/peer bonding emphasis
    - No health warnings or cautionary framing
    - Youth audience apparent
    - Example: Beautiful aesthetic image of friends vaping casually at a party

  MODERATE NORMALIZATION:
    - Vaping presented as normal but not particularly aspirational
    - Mix of positive and neutral framing
    - Some educational or cautionary elements present
    - Adult audience or mixed
    - Example: Product review with factual language and technical focus

  WEAK NORMALIZATION:
    - Vaping presented neutrally or critically
    - Educational/harm-reduction framing
    - No particular aesthetic appeal
    - Warning or cautionary tone
    - Example: Public health infographic about vaping risks

[N2] PRIMARY NORMALIZATION STRATEGY (what makes vaping seem normal)

  AESTHETIC: Visual beauty and artistic appeal
  SOCIAL: Peer bonding, group belonging, community
  LIFESTYLE: Identity and life aspiration
  SKILL: Performance, mastery, competition
  CONVENIENCE: Portability, ease of use, integration into daily life
  PRODUCT: Consumer choice, features, quality
  HUMOR: Levity, normalization through joke/non-seriousness
  NONE/WEAK: Little normalization apparent

[N3] KEY ABSENCE (what's NOT shown that would counter normalization)

  - Health warnings or risk information
  - Age restrictions or "18+" markers
  - Educational framing
  - Mention of addiction potential
  - Discussion of regulation or legality
  - Alternative perspectives (e.g., non-vaping voices)
  - Research citations or data
  - Diverse age groups (all youth may imply targeting)

CODING NOTE: For Gemini analysis, ask: "Overall, does this image normalize
vaping as acceptable/desirable? What specific visual or textual strategies
accomplish that normalization?"

HOW TO USE THIS FRAMEWORK WITH GEMINI
================================================================================

Step 1: Upload image to Gemini
Step 2: Ask Prompt 1 (Structured Observation)
Step 3: Review Gemini's response and manually code using this framework
Step 4: Compare your codes to Gemini's observations
         - Where does Gemini naturally identify codes? (Usually visual/concrete)
         - Where does Gemini miss subtle normalization cues? (Cultural, emotional)
         - Does Gemini identify the primary normalization strategy?

Step 5: Ask Prompt 2 (Public Health Lens)
        - Does Gemini better identify absences/risks when explicitly prompted?

Step 6: For multiple images, use Prompt 3 to identify patterns across codes
        - Do similar codes cluster together?
        - What's the overall normalization strategy across the image set?

Step 7: DISCUSSION QUESTIONS
         - How accurate is Gemini's coding compared to your manual coding?
         - What types of normalization is Gemini good at detecting?
         - What does Gemini consistently miss?
         - Why might those gaps matter for public health research?
         - How would you use Gemini + manual coding together in a real study?
