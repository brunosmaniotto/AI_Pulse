EXTRACTION SCHEMA: Campus Food Access Survey
==============================================

For each survey response, extract and classify according to the following schema:

1. BARRIER TYPE (Primary classification, required)
   - Cost: Financial constraints, unaffordable meal plans, pricing concerns
   - Time: Schedule conflicts, time management, preparation time limitations
   - Dietary: Dietary restrictions, allergies, cultural/religious food requirements
   - Location: Distance, commute, physical access, campus location
   - Stigma: Shame, reluctance to ask for help, feeling unwelcome
   - Information: Lack of knowledge about resources, poor advertising
   - Schedule: Work conflicts, class schedule incompatibility with meal times
   - Other: Barriers not fitting above categories

2. LEVEL (Multilevel analysis framework, required)
   - Intrapersonal: Individual knowledge, skills, beliefs, emotional barriers (e.g., shame, lack of awareness)
   - Interpersonal: Social interactions, relationships, discrimination, peer influence
   - Institutional: University policies, resource availability, accessibility, system design

3. SENTIMENT (Emotional tone of response, required)
   - Positive: Grateful, satisfied, hopeful
   - Negative: Frustrated, angry, distressed
   - Mixed: Acknowledges both positives and negatives
   - Neutral: Factual, matter-of-fact, no clear emotional valence

4. SPECIFIC RESOURCES MENTIONED (List any, optional)
   - Campus dining locations (e.g., dining halls, Crossroads, specific residence halls)
   - Campus services (e.g., food pantry, meal plans)
   - Off-campus resources mentioned
   - Leave blank if none mentioned

5. KEY ENTITIES (People, places, programs referenced, optional)
   - Types: campus locations (building names, residence areas), role positions (peer mentor, dining workers), programs (CAPS, food pantry)
   - List each distinct entity
   - Leave blank if none explicitly mentioned

6. URGENCY (Severity/acuteness of need, required)
   - Low: Student reports manageable situation, adequate access
   - Medium: Student reports moderate barriers with workarounds or partial solutions
   - High: Student reports acute food insecurity, significant health/wellbeing impact

OUTPUT FORMAT
=============

Tab-separated values preferred. Columns in order:
Response_ID | Barrier_Type | Level | Sentiment | Resources_Mentioned | Key_Entities | Urgency
