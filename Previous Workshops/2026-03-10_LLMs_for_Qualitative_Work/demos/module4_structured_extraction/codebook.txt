CODEBOOK: Campus Food Access Barriers
=====================================

THEME 1: COST BARRIERS
-----------------------
Definition: Financial constraints preventing adequate food access; includes unaffordable meal plans, expensive dining options, limited discretionary spending on food, inability to purchase food at market prices.

Indicators:
- Explicit mention of "can't afford," "expensive," "too costly," "financial"
- References to limited budget allocation to food
- Statements about choosing food-related sacrifices to save money
- Discussion of food from low-cost sources (ramen, vending machines, convenience stores)

Example: "I honestly can't afford to eat out much, and the dining plans are way too expensive for what you get." (R1)

---

THEME 2: TIME AND SCHEDULE BARRIERS
------------------------------------
Definition: Temporal constraints preventing food access; includes work-school scheduling conflicts, timing mismatches with meal service, time demands leaving insufficient time for food preparation or procurement.

Indicators:
- Explicit mention of "schedule," "time," "busy," "hours"
- References to meal times conflicting with work/class/research commitments
- Statements about insufficient time for food preparation
- Work shift conflicts with dining hall hours

Example: "By the time I finish my shift at the library and get to the dining hall, it's closed." (R2)

---

THEME 3: DIETARY AND CULTURAL BARRIERS
---------------------------------------
Definition: Dietary restrictions, medical needs, or cultural/religious food requirements unmet by available options; includes allergies, religious dietary laws, medical conditions, cultural food expectations.

Indicators:
- Explicit mention of "kosher," "halal," "diabetes," "allergies," "vegetarian," "cultural"
- References to specific dietary needs or restrictions
- Statements about limited food options matching identity or medical requirements
- Need for specific preparation methods

Example: "The food options don't accommodate my kosher needs, so I end up having to cook a lot at home." (R3)

---

THEME 4: LOCATION AND ACCESS BARRIERS
--------------------------------------
Definition: Physical distance, location constraints, or accessibility issues preventing timely food access; includes commute distance, off-campus living, geographic isolation from resources, transportation challenges.

Indicators:
- Explicit mention of "distance," "location," "commute," "far," "off-campus"
- References to time spent traveling to access food
- Statements about geographic inconvenience or isolation
- Living situation affecting access logistics

Example: "I live off-campus and commute from Oakland, so by the time I get to campus, eat somewhere, and get back, it's half my day." (R6)

---

THEME 5: STIGMA AND AWARENESS BARRIERS
---------------------------------------
Definition: Social or psychological barriers including shame, reluctance to seek help, feeling unwelcome, or lack of information about available resources; includes awareness gaps about what exists.

Indicators:
- Explicit mention of "stigma," "unwelcome," "didn't know," "ashamed," "didn't realize"
- References to emotional discomfort accessing resources
- Statements about discrimination or poor treatment by staff
- Information discovery narratives ("I didn't know until...")
- Psychological barriers to requesting help

Example: "I didn't realize the university has a food pantry until junior year! If someone had told me about it earlier, it would have made such a difference. The stigma of asking for help was real." (R5)

---

THEME 6: INSTITUTIONAL SUPPORT GAPS
------------------------------------
Definition: System-level failures including inadequate resources, poor service design, inequitable policies, insufficient graduate student support, limited communication about available services.

Indicators:
- References to insufficient resources or options
- Statements about institutional policy inequities
- Comments on system design failures
- Criticism of communication or advertising
- Comparison to programs/benefits for other groups
- Worker behavior or institutional culture issues

Example: "I'm a grad student and don't have access to the undergrad meal plans. The on-campus food is expensive and there's no subsidized option for graduate students." (R13)

---

CODING RULES
============

1. Each response receives ONE primary theme code but may contain elements of multiple themes. Code the dominant barrier.

2. If multiple barriers are equally emphasized, use this hierarchy (primary to secondary):
   - Stigma/Awareness → Cost → Time → Location → Dietary → Institutional

3. If a response primarily describes system critique without describing personal experience, code as Institutional Support Gaps.

4. Sarcasm and irony should be interpreted for actual barrier type (R4 is a Cost barrier despite the sarcastic tone).

5. Positive responses or minimal barriers may still receive codes based on context clues (e.g., "I'm fine" but acknowledging luck indicates awareness of cost barriers for peers).
