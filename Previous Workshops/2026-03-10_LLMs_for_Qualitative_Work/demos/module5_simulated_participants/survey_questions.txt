SURVEY: Experiences with Campus Mental Health Services
=======================================================

Thank you for taking the time to share your experience with mental health support at UC Berkeley.
Your feedback will help us understand how to better serve our community.

---

Q1: Describe your experience trying to access mental health support at UC Berkeley.

(Open response. Please share your story — what prompted you to seek help, how did you find resources, what was the process like?)

---

Q2: What barriers, if any, have you encountered in getting the help you needed?

(Open response. Consider barriers related to: time, cost, availability, cultural fit, transportation, stigma, information gaps, etc. If you haven't encountered barriers, you can share what made access easy for you.)

---

Q3: How do you think the university could better support student mental health?

(Open response. What changes would make the biggest difference? Think broadly — institutional policies, resource availability, cultural shifts, peer support, etc.)

---

Q4: Has your cultural background or identity shaped how you approach mental health? If so, how?

(Open response. This could include race, ethnicity, gender identity, sexual orientation, religion, class background, immigration status, family culture, etc. — whatever feels relevant to you.)

---

Q5: What would an ideal campus mental health system look like to you?

(Open response. Imagine Berkeley's mental health ecosystem as you'd most want it. What would exist? How would it work? Who would lead? What values would guide it?)

---

SURVEY METADATA
===============

Duration: 10-15 minutes
Format: Open-ended responses (no character limit)
Anonymity: Responses are confidential; no identifying information required
