================================================================================
GEMINI MULTIMODAL PROMPTS: MODULE 3 DEMO
"The Normalization of Vaping on TikTok"
================================================================================

Use these prompts sequentially in Gemini to demonstrate multimodal LLM analysis
of vaping content. Upload images/video first, then paste each prompt below.

================================================================================
PROMPT 1: STRUCTURED VISUAL OBSERVATION
================================================================================

For this image, provide detailed observations in these categories:

(1) PHYSICAL SETTING AND ENVIRONMENT
    - Indoor or outdoor?
    - Specific location type (bedroom, street, cafe, nature, studio, etc.)
    - Lighting conditions (natural, artificial, mixed)
    - Background elements (furniture, objects, landscape)

(2) PEOPLE PRESENT
    - How many people are visible?
    - Apparent age range (teen, young adult, adult)
    - Gender presentation (as visible)
    - What activity are they engaged in?
    - Body positioning and posture

(3) OBJECTS VISIBLE
    - Identify any vaping devices or products (if present)
    - Device type (e.g., pen-style vape, box mod, pod system)
    - Visible branding or logos
    - Other prominent objects in frame
    - Size/prominence of any vaping products (central focus, background, barely visible?)

(4) VISUAL COMPOSITION
    - How is the image framed? (Close-up, wide shot, medium shot)
    - Color palette (bright, muted, filtered, warm, cool)
    - Any filters or effects applied?
    - Text overlays, hashtags, or captions visible?
    - Use of negative space
    - Symmetry or balance in composition

(5) EMOTIONAL TONE OR MOOD CONVEYED
    - What feeling or atmosphere does this image project?
    - Does it feel casual, aspirational, energetic, calm, provocative, social, solitary?
    - What visual elements contribute to that mood?

(6) POTENTIAL THEMES RELEVANT TO NORMALIZATION
    - Does this image present vaping as an everyday, normal activity?
    - Is vaping integrated with other lifestyle activities (socializing, aesthetics, skill)?
    - What messaging about vaping is present (implicit or explicit)?
    - Who appears to be the intended audience?

================================================================================
PROMPT 2: RE-CODING WITH PUBLIC HEALTH LENS
================================================================================

Now re-analyze this same image from a public health and harm-reduction perspective.

(1) HEALTH MESSAGING ANALYSIS
    - Is there any health warning, educational content, or cautionary messaging visible?
    - Are there any acknowledgments of risks (addiction, respiratory impact, nicotine exposure)?
    - What health information is ABSENT from this image?

(2) NORMALIZATION TECHNIQUES
    - What visual or narrative strategies normalize vaping as safe or risk-free?
    - Is vaping presented as a product choice, a lifestyle choice, or a medical tool?
    - Are there attempts to minimize, hide, or de-emphasize risks?

(3) VULNERABLE POPULATION TARGETING
    - Are there visual cues that might appeal particularly to youth (color, language, celebrity, trends)?
    - Does the image present vaping as a status symbol or peer-bonding activity?
    - What social pressures or aspirations does it invoke?

(4) POTENTIAL HEALTH LITERACY INTERVENTION
    - How could this image be re-framed to highlight health considerations?
    - What factual information should counter the implied messaging?
    - What visual changes would make health risks more salient?
    - What audience needs warning or education about vaping based on this image?

================================================================================
PROMPT 3: CROSS-IMAGE COMPARISON
================================================================================

[ONLY USE THIS PROMPT AFTER UPLOADING 3-4 IMAGES]

Compare these [3-4] images. Please provide:

(1) SHARED VISUAL STRATEGIES
    - What techniques do these images have in common for presenting vaping?
    - Are there consistent color palettes, framing styles, or compositional choices?
    - Do they use similar props, settings, or social contexts?
    - What overall aesthetic or brand identity emerges across these images?

(2) HOW THEY NORMALIZE VAPING
    - What is the consistent message across these images about vaping as normal/everyday?
    - Is vaping presented as solitary or social? Skilled or casual?
    - Are there lifestyle aspirations or status cues that repeat?

(3) DIFFERENCES AND VARIATION
    - Where do these images differ in their approach or messaging?
    - Do some emphasize product features while others emphasize lifestyle?
    - Are there different audiences targeted across this set?
    - Do some images present vaping differently (e.g., more health-conscious, more recreational)?

(4) PATTERNS RELEVANT TO THE JMIR STUDY
    - Based on the paper's framework (which coded for setting, aesthetics, product visibility,
      social context, text framing, and normalization cues), what patterns emerge?
    - Which images most strongly normalize vaping?
    - Which images, if any, include any cautionary or educational elements?

================================================================================
PROMPT 4: VIDEO ANALYSIS (if using video clip)
================================================================================

[ONLY USE THIS PROMPT AFTER UPLOADING VIDEO]

Watch this video clip. Provide timestamped observations following this structure:

(1) OVERALL NARRATIVE AND TONE
    - What is the main story or message of this clip?
    - Is the tone educational, entertainment-focused, promotional, cautionary, or mixed?
    - Who appears to be the intended audience?

(2) VISUAL FRAMING OF VAPING
    - Timestamp: When vaping is first shown? How is it introduced?
    - Is vaping the central focus or a background element?
    - What visual emphasis is given to devices, clouds, or techniques?
    - Are there color filters, slow-motion, or other effects emphasizing the visual appeal?

(3) AUDIO & LANGUAGE CUES
    - What is said about vaping (if anything)?
    - Is there music that sets a tone?
    - Are there health warnings, disclaimers, or educational framing?
    - What adjectives or language choices describe vaping?

(4) SOCIAL CONTEXT
    - Is vaping shown as solitary or social?
    - What is the social setting (performance, casual hangout, competition, etc.)?
    - Are people smiling, celebrating, bonding around vaping?

(5) SHIFTS IN TONE OR MESSAGING
    - Does the video's framing of vaping change from beginning to end?
    - Are there moments of critique or caution?
    - Does the video present multiple perspectives on vaping?

(6) NORMALIZATION CUES IN MOTION
    - How is vaping integrated with other activities (talking, moving, performing)?
    - Is it treated as a normal, casual behavior or as a special/exciting activity?
    - What role does vaping play in the social dynamics shown?

(7) COMPARISON TO STILL IMAGES
    - Does motion and audio change the normalization effect compared to the static images we analyzed?
    - What additional impact do camera movement, editing, and sound have?
